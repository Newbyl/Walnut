ArithmeticOperator.java

Act()

Checks if the expression stack S has enough expressions to match the arity of the Operator. If so, it unstacks one (named b) or two expressions (named b and a) depending on the arity of the operator. It then operates differently depending on the types of the unstacked expressions: If the operator is "_" (unary minus) and b is of type:

  • Number Literal : Changes the literal to its opposite, add a "-" before its name and changes its value to the opposite and adds it to the expression Stack S
  • Alphabet Letter : Changes the value of the letter to its opposite and adds it to the expression Stack S
  • Word : It applies the operator on the automaton of the Word and adds it to the expression Stack S
If the operator's arity is 2 ( "+", "-", "*", "/" ), and a and b are of type :
  • Word and Word : Applies the operator using both automaton W of words and concatenates their M automaton and adds the indentifiers of b to the ones of a.Then adds a to S
  • Word and (Alphabet Letter or Number Literal) : Applies the operator to the W automaton of Word and the constant. Adds the Word to the expression Stack S
  • Both are (Alphabet Letter or Number Literal) : Adds to the stack of expression S a String which represents the formula : a operator b
If it is another combination, it creates a new expression which is a string representing "(a operator b)" with an automaton associated then adds it to the expression stack S.
